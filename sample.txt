Abstract
Grokkingdelayed generalization long after memorizationis controlled by weight norm
dynamics. We show that a simple intervention, per-row weight norm clipping on decoder
layers, accelerates grokking by 39249× across six algebraic tasksfour modular arithmetic
operations, their composition, and S5 permutationwhile eliminating the need for weight
decay. The method requires a few lines of code and no additional memory overhead.
We provide an analysis connecting weight norm clipping to four established frameworks:
(i) Omnigrok's Goldilocks zone timescale, which collapses when norms are clamped to w ≈
wc; (ii) the α
L depth scaling law, which motivates our edge initialization strategy for deep
models; (iii) sign-based optimizers' uniform ±lr updates, which combine naturally with ℓ2
clipping to stabilize training across learning rates; and (iv) Softmax Collapse prevention,
which eliminates the post-memorization logit growth that stalls standard training. Edge
initializationnormalizing only embeddings, the nal LayerNorm, and the output head
achieves zero failures across 300 runs on 8-layer architectures and reduces interquartile range
by 6172%.
We observe that Grokfast's gradient EMA lter, when passed through Adam's secondmoment normalization, produces updates that approximate sign-based optimizers like Lion
suggesting that prior acceleration methods may have succeeded partly through implicit
norm-constrained dynamics.
1 Introduction
Grokkingthe phenomenon where neural networks generalize long after memorizing training
datawas rst documented by Power et al. [2022] on small algorithmic datasets. The delay
between memorization and generalization can span tens of thousands of optimization steps,
raising fundamental questions about the dynamics of neural network learning. Subsequent work
has converged on a central insight: the grokking delay is controlled by weight norm dynamics.
Liu et al. [2023] showed that generalization concentrates in a Goldilocks zonea narrow
spherical shell in weight spaceand that constraining weight norms to this zone nearly eliminates grokking. Lee et al. [2024] approached the same problem from the gradient perspective,
amplifying slow-varying gradient components to accelerate generalization by over 50×. Prieto
et al. [2025] revealed that unconstrained weight growth leads to Softmax Collapse, a oatingpoint failure mode that stalls generalization entirely. Each line of work arrives at the same
conclusion from a dierent angle: controlling weight magnitude is the key to fast generalization.
We take the most direct path: clipping each decoder layer's weight row norms to a small
constant after every optimizer step. With no additional memory overhead, this achieves 39249×
speedup across six algebraic tasks, including 66× on modular multiplication (2-layer) and 18×
on 8-layer architectures (1.6M parameters), while eliminating post-convergence instability and
the need for weight decay. The method works across optimizersAdam, Lion, and SignSGD
with Lion+Clip achieving the strongest results and notably stabilizing Lion's typically narrow
learning rate tolerance. Figure 1 illustrates the eect on a single 2-layer run.

Hyperparameter trade-o. The method replaces weight decaya single value requiring

modular multiplication) and an initialization pattern (all for shallow models, edge_ln for deep).
Neither requires per-run tuning: max_norm is xed across all optimizers, architectures, and
learning rates tested; the init pattern is determined by depth alone. In exchange, weight decay
and its schedule are eliminated entirely.

(1) weight decay becomes unnecessaryclipping keeps norms in the zone from the start;
(2) robustness to exact cthe method works with max_norm= 2.0 across all tested congurations without tuning, suggesting the Goldilocks zone is broad relative to the clipping threshold.
Remark 1. Clipping is strictly stronger than weight decay for norm control. Weight decay
applies w ← (1 − λη) w, requiring many steps. Clipping enforces ∥w∥ ≤ c in a single projection.

4.4 Preventing Softmax Collapse
Prieto et al. [2025] identied Softmax Collapse (SC): after memorization, the training objective (negative log-likelihood) can still decrease by increasing logit magnitudes, which requires
growing weight norms. This post-memorization weight growth drives logit amplication until oat32 overow causes absorption errors in softmax, producing erratic gradients that stall
generalization.
Clipping eliminates this cascade by bounding decoder norms: ∥wrow∥2 ≤ c directly caps logit
growth proportional to c, preventing overow regardless of training duration.
Remark 2 (Weight decay vs. clipping for SC). Weight decay prevents SC eventually; clipping
prevents it immediatelyeliminating the plateau-then-grokking pattern.
5 Related Work
The grokking phenomenon. Power et al. [2022] rst observed delayed generalization. Nanda
et al. [2023] provided mechanistic interpretability, identifying Fourier multiplication circuits and
three training phases.
Weight norm and the Goldilocks zone. Liu et al. [2023] showed grokking occurs when
initialized outside the zone. Lyu et al. [2024] proved the kernel-to-max-margin transition at
t ∼ (1/λ) ln α. Our method operationalizes these insights via per-row clipping.

Depth and logit scaling. Vysogorets et al. [2024] connected weight scale to α
L logit ampli-
cation. Fan et al. [2024] examined grokking vs. depth but didn't propose norm control.
Numerical stability. Prieto et al. [2025] identied Softmax Collapse. Their ⊥Grad modies
gradients; we prevent weight growth directly.
Gradient-based acceleration. Lee et al. [2024] proposed Grokfast (> 50× speedup). We
observe that its EMA lter, combined with Adam's normalization, produces approximately signbased updates (Section 4.3). NeuralGrok [Zhou et al., 2025] learns a neural gradient lter but
doesn't transfer.
Normalized representations. nGPT [Loshchilov et al., 2025] normalizes all vectors to unit
norm for 420× LM speedup. The connection between our weight-space clipping and their
representation-space normalization warrants investigation. Spectral normalization [Miyato et al.,
2018] constrains the operator norm of each weight matrix (largest singular value), controlling
worst-case input amplication. This is a complementary constraint: spectral norm bounds
how much a layer can amplify any input direction, while per-row clipping bounds individual
neuron magnitudes. The two are not interchangeablea matrix can satisfy one constraint while
violating the other. GrokTransfer [Xu et al., 2025] uses embedding transfer (46-step gap).
Karras et al. [2024] independently applied per-row weight normalization in EDM2, redesigning diusion model layers to preserve activation, weight, and update magnitudes on expectation
achieving state-of-the-art image synthesis quality. Their approach forces each weight row to a
xed target norm √
N, whereas our clipping caps norms at c (allowing smaller values). Despite
this dierence, the shared principlebounding per-row weight magnitude to stabilize training
emerging independently in two domains (algorithmic generalization and image diusion) suggests
it may reect a general property of gradient-based optimization.
Sign-based optimization. Lion [Chen et al., 2023] solves ℓ∞-constrained optimization [Chen
et al., 2024]. Sfyraki & Wang [2025] unied Lion/Muon under FrankWolfe. Tveit et al. [2025]
showed Muon accelerates grokking via spectral norm control.
6 Conclusion
Weight norm clipping accelerates grokking by 39249× across six algebraic tasks (modular arithmetic and S5 permutation composition), with no additional memory overhead. Four established
frameworksOmnigrok timescale collapse, α
L depth scaling, constrained optimization, and SC
preventioneach independently predict this outcome. Edge initialization eliminates all failures
across 300 runs and reduces IQR by 6172%. The method spans two orders of magnitude in
model size (9.8k1.6M parameters) without modication.
The near-simultaneous train/val convergence suggests the traditional memorization-generalization
gap may be an artifact of unconstrained optimization. Lion+Clip emerges as the recommended
conguration: fastest convergence, tightest seed variance, widest LR tolerance, and the only
optimizer that generalizes reliably under data-scarce conditions (25/75 split).
Limitations. All experiments use small algorithmic tasks: four modular arithmetic operations
(+, −, ×, ÷ mod 97), a composite task (all four operations jointly), and S5 permutation composition. While results hold across model scales (9.8k1.6M parameters), depths (28 layers),
optimizers, and training congurations, we have not validated on natural language or other domains. These tasks are the standard benchmarks for grokking research, but the practical value of
accelerated grokking depends on whether the same weight norm dynamics govern generalization
in larger-scale settings.
15
Future work. The most direct extension is language model pretraining with norm clipping,
particularly in combination with nGPT's representation-space normalization [Loshchilov et al.,
2025]. If post-step clipping can replace weight decay scheduling at scaleas it replaces weight
decay entirely in our experimentsthe practical impact would be substantial. The method's
compatibility with sign-based optimizers (Lion, SignSGD) is especially relevant given their lower
memory footprint for large models.
